Campus Life

Belonging is built. Leadership is practiced. Joy is essential.

Arts

Music, strings, studio art, digital media, and theater craft. Students create, perform, and exhibit with real audiences and clear rubrics.

  • Choir and instrumental studies
  • Drawing, painting, and sculpture
  • Digital design and film projects
  • Stagecraft and production roles

Athletics and Wellness

Strength, stamina, skill, and sportsmanship. Training scales by age and focuses on lifelong health and confident movement.

  • Cross-country and track sessions
  • Tennis, swimming, and court skills
  • Conditioning and mobility blocks
  • Outdoor fitness challenges

Clubs and Leadership

Students lead, plan, and deliver. Clubs start from a proposal, a purpose, and a calendar.

  • Robotics and coding studio
  • Speech and Debate
  • Model UN and Civics
  • Entrepreneurship and service council

Service and Outdoor Education

We learn by serving and by being outside. Students complete age-appropriate projects that help people and care for places.

  • Neighborhood and park cleanups
  • Food bank or drives by season
  • Trail days and nature journaling
  • Orienteering and field skills

Daily Rhythm

Order supports freedom. A steady day builds focus and joy.

  • Morning meeting and goal setting
  • Focused work blocks with breaks
  • Seminars, studios, and labs
  • Exercise and outdoor time
  • Review, reflection, and reading

Phones and personal devices are away during study unless approved for learning.

Belonging and Conduct

We protect a safe, orderly, and joyful environment. See our standards for the habits that make this possible.
